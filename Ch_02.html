Chapter 2: Inventory & Classification

The Identification Audit

Use this checklist to decide if an asset can qualify as a trade secret.

1. Information

  • Formula, pattern, compilation, method, process
  • Source code, manufacturing settings, customer insights
  • Not just general employee skill

2. Economic Value

  • Creates measurable advantage
  • Supports profit, efficiency, or market position
  • Not routine administrative information

3. Not Generally Known

  • Not broadly known in the field
  • Not publicly disclosed
  • Can still be shared under NDA

4. Not Readily Ascertainable

  • Cannot be easily reconstructed by honest means
  • Reverse engineering risk is considered
  • Trivial discovery weakens protection

5. Reasonable Efforts

  • Contracts and NDAs in place
  • Access controls and confidentiality labels
  • Consistent security practices

Classification Hub

Secret Categories

Engineering / R&D
Technical: formulas, process steps, source code.

Sales / Ops
Business: pricing models, customer lists, supplier terms.

Marketing Tech
Hybrid: integrated data + algorithms.

Priority Tiers

  • High (Crown Jewels): catastrophic harm if disclosed.
  • Medium (Operational): replaceable but costly to lose.
  • Low (Administrative/Legacy): still confidential, lower litigation value.
