Protecting Trade Secrets

Chapter 3: Risk & Vulnerability

Sources of Exposure

Map the most common ways secrets leak, and use the case hooks to teach the “why.”

Internal

Insiders

  • Departing employees (PepsiCo v. Redmond)
  • Negligence (personal email forwarding)
  • Lack of training / low awareness
  • Unsuitable contractor incentives

Lesson: Create off-boarding scripts and track “inevitable disclosure” risks.

External

Partners

  • Vendors with access to code or data
  • Joint venture design sharing
  • Consultants and outsourced teams
  • Loose or missing NDAs (nClosures v. Block)

Lesson: Classify collaborators and flow down confidentiality obligations.

Systems

Infrastructure

  • Unsecured remote access / no VPN
  • Weak segmentation or logging
  • Phishing and malware aimed at I.P.
  • Public Wi‑Fi use by remote teams

Lesson: Map who can access what, then shrink the blast radius.
