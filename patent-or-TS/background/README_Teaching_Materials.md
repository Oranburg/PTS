### Detailed Case Coverage:

**LEVEL 1: Clear Non-Protectable Cases (20 min)**
- 1A: BioPharma FDA Disclosure → Cannot maintain trade secret; should patent
- 1B: Customer List → Cannot patent; may be trade secret
- 1C: Abstract Software Algorithm → Likely not patentable (Alice); trade secret depends on deployment

**LEVEL 2: Clear Strategic Choices (30 min)**
- 2A: Chemical Sweetener → Must patent (easy to reverse engineer)
- 2B: Client-Side JavaScript → Should patent or redesign (code distribution defeats trade secret)
- 2C: Bioreactor Process → Should maintain as trade secret (difficult to reverse engineer, internal use)

**LEVEL 3: Difficult Strategic Decisions (40 min)**
- 3A: Soft Drink Formula (Coca-Cola) → Trade secret was correct (140 years vs. 20 years)
- 3B: Search Algorithm (Google) → Hybrid approach optimal (patent core + trade secret implementation)
- 3C: Pharmaceutical Process → Complex tradeoff; hybrid likely optimal (no single right answer)

**LEVEL 4: Technology Stack Cases (30 min)**
- 4A: Biotech Platform → Component-by-component mixed strategy (patents, trade secrets, copyright, trademark, MTAs)
- 4B: AI Analytics Platform → SaaS-specific strategy; consider open-source for periphery

## HOW TO USE THESE MATERIALS

### Before Class:
1. **Review the Instructor's Guide** (Word doc) thoroughly
2. **Print or keep handy** on a small screen during class
3. **Review your Chapter 2 materials** to ensure alignment
4. **Practice Socratic questions** for a few cases

### During Class:
1. **Project the PowerPoint** for students to see facts
2. **Use Socratic method** following the guide's suggested questions
3. **Reference the Instructor's Guide** for:
   - Additional facts to elaborate if needed
   - Answer guidance and reasoning
   - Common mistakes to watch for
   - Discussion extension questions
4. **Advance through slides** as you complete each case

### Suggested Flow for Each Case:
1. Display slide with student facts
2. Ask opening Socratic question
3. Let students discuss and respond
4. Provide additional facts if needed (from guide)
5. Guide discussion with follow-up questions
6. Reveal answer and reasoning
7. Emphasize key teaching points
8. If time permits, use discussion extensions

### Time Management:
- **Introduction:** 10 minutes (review Chapter 2 doctrine)
- **Level 1:** 20 minutes (3 cases × ~7 min)
- **Level 2:** 30 minutes (3 cases × 10 min)
- **Break:** 10 minutes
- **Level 3:** 40 minutes (3 cases × 13 min)
- **Level 4:** 30 minutes (2 cases × 15 min)
- **Wrap-up:** 10 minutes (key takeaways)

**Total:** 2 hours (120 minutes)

*Adjust timing based on class engagement - some cases may generate rich discussion*

## PEDAGOGICAL APPROACH

### Socratic Method:
- Present facts incrementally
- Ask questions before revealing answers
- Encourage student reasoning
- Validate thoughtful analysis even when conclusions differ

### No Single Right Answer (Level 3-4):
- Multiple defensible positions exist
- Focus on quality of reasoning
- Reward consideration of multiple factors
- Encourage debate and discussion

### Real-World Grounding:
- Emphasize these are based on actual cases
- Connect to current industry practices
- Reference peer-reviewed research

### Progressive Skill Building:
- Start simple (clear doctrinal limitations)
- Build to technical analysis (reverse engineering)
- Advance to strategic considerations (market position)
- Culminate in complex integrated strategies (technology stacks)
