Case 3A: The Soft Drink Formula

Global Beverages Inc. (1886-Present)

The Situation

  • Unique soft drink formula developed in 1886 with specific proportions of natural ingredients
  • Product has become iconic brand with massive consumer loyalty (40 percent market share)
  • Reverse engineering difficult but not impossible with modern analytical chemistry
  • Patent filed in 1886 would have expired in 1906 (20 years)
  • Formula maintained as trade secret for 140 years; only two executives know complete formula
  • Brand value: $80 billion; even if reverse engineered, trademark provides protection
  • Mature industry with slow-changing consumer preferences

Was trade secret the right choice in 1886? Would the same decision make sense today?
